Research Statement

My research lies at the intersection of Mathematical Optimization, Machine Learning, and Signal Processing. I am particularly interested in how principled optimization techniques—both convex and non-convex—can improve learning stability, generalization, and computational efficiency in high-dimensional data settings.

I focus on image, audio, and speech signal processing, studying representation learning, feature extraction, and structured modeling techniques for real-world signals. My work explores how optimization-driven frameworks can enhance interpretability and robustness in machine learning systems, especially when dealing with noisy or limited data.

With a strong foundation in Linear Algebra, Probability, Statistics, and Signals & Systems, I aim to develop theoretically grounded and computationally efficient models that bridge modern machine learning with classical signal processing theory.
